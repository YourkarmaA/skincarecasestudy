6 Root Cause Findings from the Audit
01
Zero Funnel Segmentation — Everything Running in One Campaign
Cold traffic (Broad/Interest), warm audiences (Video viewers, Website visitors), and retargeting (ATC/IC) were all in the same campaign with the same creative and same bid. Meta couldn't optimise properly because users at completely different purchase stages were competing in the same auction against each other. The algorithm was delivering the budget to the cheapest impressions — not the highest intent.
🔴 Critical — Primary ROAS killer
02
Creative Fatigue — Same 3 Ads Running for 4 Months
The account had only 3 active ad creatives. All three were static images with the same product shot, same copy format, and no variation in hook, format, or angle. Average frequency across active ad sets was 7.2 — meaning each user had seen the same ad more than 7 times. CTR had fallen from 1.4% (Month 1) to 0.61% by August. No creative testing framework existed.
🔴 Critical — Causing CPM inflation and CTR collapse
03
Interest Stacking — Overly Narrow, Overlapping Audiences
Ad sets were using 6–9 stacked interests per ad set (e.g., "Skincare + Beauty + Dermatology + Organic Beauty + Sephora + Nykaa + L'Oreal…"), creating audience sizes under 800K in Tier-1 cities. Multiple ad sets were cannibalising each other with 60–70% audience overlap. This inflated CPM and caused the algorithm to never exit the learning phase (under 50 conversions/week per ad set).
🟠 High — Preventing algorithm optimisation
04
Landing Page Disconnect — Ad Promised One Thing, Page Delivered Another
The best-performing ad was a UGC-style video focused on "glass skin in 14 days" — but the landing page it drove to was a generic product listing page with no mention of that claim, no social proof, and a slow load time (4.8s LCP on mobile). This created a sharp funnel drop between landing page visit and ATC. The conversion issue wasn't fully in the ads — it was the post-click experience.
🟠 High — Destroying CVR after click
05
Budget Allocated by Gut, Not by Data — No Scaling Logic
Budget increases were being made weekly without any defined threshold. Ad sets that had ROAS of 0.8x were kept running "to give them more time," while ad sets hitting 5x ROAS were never scaled because the founder was worried about breaking them. No documented SOP existed for when to pause, when to scale, or how much to increase budgets.
🟡 Medium — Suboptimal allocation
06
Pixel Setup Had Duplicate Events and Missing Purchase Signals
The Meta Pixel was firing ViewContent twice on the product page (due to a theme conflict), which was inflating upper-funnel signals and confusing the algorithm. More critically, the Purchase event was only set up via the pixel and had no Conversions API (CAPI) backup — meaning iOS 14+ users were entirely invisible. Approximately 35% of purchases were likely unattributed, which made ROAS reporting worse than reality.
🟡 Medium — Underreporting real performance
The Fix

Strategy & Execution Framework

The rebuild was executed in three phases over the first 8 weeks — first fixing tracking, then rebuilding campaign structure, then launching a creative testing engine.

"Fix what's broken before you spend more. Every rupee spent on a broken account is a rupee teaching the algorithm the wrong thing."

Creative Engine

Creative Testing Framework

We moved from 3 static assets to a systematic weekly creative testing cadence — 15–20 new ads per week, each testing one variable at a time. Every creative was named, tracked, and tagged with performance outcomes.

Creative Naming Convention
SG_[Format]_[Hook]_[Angle]_[YYYYMMDD]
SG = Skinglow Format: UGC / STAT / CAR / REEL Hook: PROB / RESULT / INGR / TESTM Angle: VITC / NIAC / SPF / BNDL
Example: SG_UGC_RESULT_VITC_20231015 → UGC video, result-based hook, Vitamin C serum angle, uploaded 15 Oct 2023

Key Creative Insight
UGC-style videos with a problem-first hook in the first 1.5 seconds consistently outperformed polished brand videos. Real-skin, no-filter content drove 3× higher CTR than product-hero shots. The audience trusted authenticity over aesthetics. Budget followed performance — no creative ran more than 14 days without a data review.
Month-by-Month

The 6-Month Execution Timeline

Each month had a clear focus area. We didn't try to fix everything at once.

Month 1 — August 2023
Audit, Pixel Fix & Tracking Rebuild
Full account audit documented in a 14-page spreadsheet. Fixed duplicate Pixel events, implemented CAPI via Shopify integration, and set up GA4 cross-referencing for attribution sanity check. No creative or campaign changes yet — fix the data layer first.
ROAS: 1.2x → 1.4x (tracking fix recapture)
Month 2 — September 2023
Campaign Restructure & Audience Rebuild
Shut down the single-campaign structure. Rebuilt 6 campaigns across the TOF/MOF/BOF funnel. Replaced stacked interests with Broad + LLA. Paused all existing creatives and briefed 20 new assets to the designer. Implemented the naming convention.
ROAS: 1.8x (new structure still in learning)
Month 3 — October 2023
Creative Testing Engine Launched
Deployed 18 new creatives across 4 formats. Established the weekly creative review cadence: every Monday, killed ads below 1% CTR after 7 days + ₹2,000 spend, scaled winners by 20% every 3 days. First UGC problem-hook video emerged as a clear winner by Week 3.
ROAS: 2.4x ↑
Month 4 — November 2023
Scale Winners + Bundle Offer Push
Scaled the 3 winning creative ad sets by 2× budget. Worked with founder to launch a "Glow Starter Kit" bundle (₹1,799) which immediately outperformed single-SKU on ROAS. Launched ASC campaign as a test — it outperformed manual campaigns in TOF within 10 days.
ROAS: 3.2x ↑
Month 5 — December 2023
Year-End Sale Sprint + BOF Retargeting Surge
December sale period (25% off site-wide). Scaled total budget to ₹9.2L for the month. Heavy BOF push on ATC/IC audiences with urgency creative ("Sale ends midnight"). ASC received 40% of budget. Monthly ROAS hit a peak of 4.8x during sale week. CPM spiked with holiday competition but was offset by CTR gains.
ROAS: 4.8x (sale period peak)
Month 6 — January 2024
Stabilise & Document — Steady-State 4.1x ROAS
Post-sale normalisation. Dialed budget back to ₹7.4L/month. Documented all SOPs, creative performance data, audience structure, and scaling rules in a full account playbook for the founder. Steady-state ROAS stabilised at 4.1x — a sustainable floor, not a peak.
ROAS: 4.1x (steady state)

What I Learned

5 Key Learnings from This Engagement

Each of these came from a specific failure, observation, or experiment — not from theory.

Learning 01
Broad audiences beat stacked interests — almost every time — in D2C skincare.
Once we stripped out interest stacking and let Meta's algorithm find purchasers in a Broad Metro audience, CPM dropped and CVR improved simultaneously. The algorithm had enough purchase signal (300+ monthly conversions) to self-optimise without us narrowing it artificially. Interest stacking is useful for new accounts with zero data — for this stage of brand, it was actively harmful.
Learning 02
Creative is the targeting — a good hook reaches the right person at the right cost.
Our "problem-first" UGC hooks (opening with the exact frustration of the audience — dark spots, uneven skin tone, SPF whitecast) effectively self-selected the right audience. CPM on these creatives was 22% lower than product-first shots because engagement signals told the algorithm: "these users respond to this content." Creative strategy IS audience strategy on Meta in 2023–24.
Learning 03
Don't optimise your way around a bad landing page — flag it, fix it.
We could have kept pushing CTR improvements, but the ATC→IC drop was a landing page problem, not an ad problem. I flagged it explicitly in the Week 3 report with data: "12% ATC rate from UGC video but only 4% IC rate — the product page is the bottleneck." The founder fixed the LP. IC rate jumped from 4% to 9% in the following week — without a single ad change. Know where your job ends and where the next problem starts.
Learning 04
Budget scaling at 20% every 3 days beats doubling overnight — every time.
We had one incident in Month 4 where I doubled a winning ad set budget based on a founder request. ROAS dropped from 5.2x to 2.8x in 48 hours as the ad set re-entered learning. We gradually restored it over 9 days. After that, the 20% rule was written into the SOP and never broken. Patience with scaling is not timidity — it's protecting the algorithm's optimisation state.
Learning 05
Daily monitoring isn't just spend-watching — it's anomaly pattern recognition.
The most valuable thing about daily checks wasn't catching overspend (that's rare) — it was catching patterns early: a CTR that had been dropping 0.1% per day for 5 days straight (creative fatigue signal), a CPM spike at 9am that self-corrected by noon (auction noise vs. real signal), a delivery drop at midnight that turned out to be a billing issue, not a policy flag. These patterns are invisible if you check weekly. They're obvious if you check daily.
